# OK Code Camp
### An interactive-making workshop, in partnership with Super-Stories

**Kids design game characters with their hands. We bring them to life. They leave with an interactive souvenir of their own creative journey.**

---

## The concept

We arrive with a *game canvas* — a complete, working game that has every mechanic but no identity: gray shapes, no story, no soul. The students fill it in. Using paper and analog materials, each child designs a character (a robot with a special power), an obstacle, and a piece of the world. Their drawings become the *skin* of a real, playable game. The mechanics are the prepared surface; the child is the artist.

## How it works

One shared game, many hands. Each student's creations combine into a single collaborative world where everyone's work lives and plays together — a robot from one child, an obstacle from another, scenery from a third. The technical building stays out of view. Children never touch code: they make the decisions and the drawings, and the machinery translates their analog work into something that moves and responds. They direct; the tool obeys.

## Why it belongs at Super-Stories

This is hands-on making and storytelling in a new medium — a game is a story you can walk around in. It centers student voice in the most literal way possible: the hero on the screen is the child's own drawing. It invites collaboration, it is safe and inclusive by design, and it extends the visual language Super-Stories already teaches into something interactive. Generative AI appears here only in service of the children's art — never in place of it.

## What the kids actually learn

Without being lectured, children discover that their imagination is the source and the computer is the instrument. They see, with their own eyes, how changing a few numbers — how fast, how high, how big — changes how their character behaves, so the machine is understood rather than mistaken for magic. The mechanism is visible, the wiring is hidden, and the authorship is unmistakably theirs. The lesson lives in the experience, not on a slide.

## What each child leaves with

A game that is theirs alone, published to a web link they can share — a keepsake of the day they made something real with their own hands, and proof that they can do it again.

## Format

- **Who:** up to 4 students
- **When:** three sessions — *design* → *build & playtest* → *polish & publish*
- **Where:** the Super-Stories Makers Space
- **Provided:** all materials and technical machinery; the workshop is turnkey for the host
