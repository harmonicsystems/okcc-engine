# OK Code Camp — Skills Framework
### A workshop designed with a clinician's experience — not therapy

OK Code Camp is a naturalistic, high-motivation context for communication and thinking. Children design a game by hand and watch it come alive, and the work of bringing *their own* creation to life is what draws out the language, the decisions, and the collaboration below. These skills are **fostered and scaffolded** in an enrichment setting; this is not therapy or a clinical service. The framework is shareable with any young creator — it is not tied to a caseload.

---

## 1 · Cognitive-communication & executive function

**In the workshop:** choosing a character and a goal; selecting traits from a bounded set of options; following multi-step and conditional directions; carrying one project across three sessions; playtesting and revising what isn't working yet.

**Skills fostered:** goal-setting and planning · decision-making under constraint · task sequencing and goal-directed persistence · following directions of increasing complexity · self-monitoring, metacognition, and cognitive flexibility (the revise-loop).

## 2 · Expressive & narrative language

**In the workshop:** naming the character; describing its traits; labeling and explaining its abilities ("*when* it touches the lava, it loses a heart"); building the story of who it is and why it's here.

**Skills fostered:** semantic-feature description (category, function, attribute) · conditional and causal language · narrative discourse and story grammar (character, setting, problem, resolution) · experience-before-label vocabulary mapping.

## 3 · Social communication & pragmatics

**In the workshop:** collaborating on one shared game; taking turns; negotiating whose idea goes where; expressing and defending a preference; giving and receiving feedback; presenting the finished game.

**Skills fostered:** turn-taking and negotiation · perspective-taking · expressing and justifying opinions · giving and receiving feedback · audience awareness and presentation.

## 4 · Access & multimodal expression

**In the workshop:** using the keyboard to play and control the game.

**Skills fostered:** interface access and agency — the keyboard as a means of control and self-expression, viewed through an AAC-informed, multimodal lens. *(The fine-motor and visual-motor demands overlap occupational therapy; this is framed as access, not as a core speech-language target.)*

---

*The unifying principle: the child's own creation is the reinforcer. Motivation drives output — a child will produce far more language, planning, and negotiation to bring their robot to life than any worksheet could elicit.*

*Harmonic Systems · communication-rich enrichment, designed with speech-language pathology experience.*
