# Make a game. Take it home.
### OK Code Camp · a creative workshop for young makers

In three sessions, your child designs and builds their very own video game — starting with nothing but paper and markers — and leaves with a real, playable game at a web link you can keep, share, and replay. No experience needed. No screens to start. Just their imagination, brought to life.

---

## What your child will do

**Dream it up, on paper.** They invent a character, give it powers, design its world and its challenges — all by hand, with markers, before a computer is ever involved.

**Bring it to life.** Their drawings become the actual game. They watch their own creation start to move, and they shape how it plays.

**Make it theirs, and share it.** The finished game goes live at a link they can send to grandparents, friends, anyone. A keepsake of the day they made something real.

## What they'll practice along the way

- **Deciding and problem-solving** — planning a character, choosing how it works, and figuring out what to change when it's not quite right yet.
- **Finding the words** — naming their character, describing what makes it special, and telling its story.
- **Working together** — building one game as a small team: sharing ideas, taking turns, giving and getting feedback.
- **Taking the controls** — playing and steering the game with their own hands.

## What they take home

A game that is unmistakably *theirs* — and the quiet confidence that comes from making something real, and knowing they could do it again.

It's a creative workshop — not therapy — built on the simple idea that kids will pour their whole imagination into something that's truly their own.*
